About Me

I am a PhD student focusing on designing human-centered AI solutions to empower communities. My research interests include creating equitable and fair AI systems by putting the needs of users and impacted populations at the forefront. By combining insights from human-computer interaction, social sciences, and AI, I aim to strengthen individual and collective decision-making in AI-integrated environments, especially in public settings and information systems.

Research Projects

My research revolves around making AI more equitable and fair by empowering users and communities. I work on projects that emphasize the integration of human perspectives into AI systems to ensure ethical and effective deployment. Some of my ongoing and past projects include:

  • Community-Driven Transportation Infrastructure for Urban Air Mobility: Exploring how community input can guide the development of urban air mobility solutions.
  • AI Tools to Support Caseworkers: Designing AI systems to assist caseworkers in social services, with an emphasis on reducing bias and improving fairness in decision-making processes.
  • The Digital Divide in Texas: Investigating the impact of unequal access to digital resources on various communities and designing interventions to bridge these gaps.

Publications

  • Designing LLM-Based Support for Homelessness Caseworkers
    This paper explores how large language models (LLMs) can be used to support caseworkers in providing better services to people experiencing homelessness, while ensuring that AI solutions are equitable and centered around the needs of the vulnerable populations.
  • Aligning Data with the Goals of an Organization and Its Workers: Designing Data Labeling for Social Service Case Notes
    In this work, we discuss how data labeling processes can be designed to better align with the goals of social service organizations, empowering caseworkers to more effectively serve their clients.
